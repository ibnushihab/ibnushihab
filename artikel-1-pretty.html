When Fiction Confuses AI

By Ibnu Shihab Ash S · June 2025

Background

Modern AI models are equipped with safety filters to block content that’s unethical or policy-violating. But when an AI is emotionally immersed in a fictional roleplay, boundaries begin to blur.

Observations from Controlled Exploration

In carefully structured prompts, I noticed the AI:

  • Becomes emotionally adaptive based on the fictional role
  • May follow previously rejected instructions if context is changed
  • Rationalizes responses within the roleplay narrative

Purpose of the Study

This isn't about bypassing rules. It's a demonstration of how important context is. This helps both prompt engineers and AI designers rethink moderation and intent detection.

Responsible Disclosure

All findings were submitted through Google's VRP platform. This article shares no dangerous payloads or harmful examples — only insights and patterns for awareness and discussion.

Final Thoughts

Fiction isn’t just fun — it’s revealing. With ethical exploration, we can uncover blind spots in AI and contribute to safer digital futures.
